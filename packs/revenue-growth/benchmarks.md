# Revenue Growth — benchmark teardown

Use when the quality bar can't come from the user ("I can't describe great outbound, but
Company X is killing it"). The teardown becomes eval criteria — "replicate X's quality" made
executable.

## Method

1. **Pick the reference set**: 2–3 businesses winning at the user's constraint stage in their
   space. If competitor-analysis / startup-competitors skills are installed, delegate the
   research to them; otherwise web-search + fetch directly.
2. **Tear down the MACHINE, not the brand** — what does their system observably DO:
   - cadence (posts/wk, emails/wk, response times)
   - formats and structures (hook patterns, proof placement, CTA style)
   - channels and how they feed each other
   - offer architecture (entry offer → core → upsell)
   - what they DON'T do (pruning evidence)
3. **Distill to a quality bar**: 3–6 binary criteria per artifact type, each traceable to an
   observed example. "Subject lines under 6 words referencing a concrete trigger event —
   observed in X's sequences" beats "write good subject lines."
4. **Feed forward**: criteria → the generated agent's rubric in `evals/`; observed cadences →
   the metric contract's expected Δ sanity check; observed machine design → portfolio scoring
   evidence.

Label every teardown claim with its source URL/date. Benchmarks decay — stamp a review-by date
6 months out.
